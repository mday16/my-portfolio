Step 4 - Set and Cycle

Now that you have all the materials necessary, you can now set up your tank in a suitable place in your home! Be sure to set up your tank in a place away from direct sunlight, away from potentially harmful pets, and accessible power outlets.

Once you have set up your tank, add all of the supplies you bought and get your filter running with your dechlorinated water. Allow at least two days for your tank to 'cycle'.

Step 5 - Test

After allowing at least two days for your tank to cycle (ammonia, nitrite, and nitrate levels are settled to 0, healthy bacteria has started in your tank, and all supplies are working) test your water parameters using a water testing kit (should at least include ph, ammonia, nitrite, and nitrate). Once you have determined that your tank levels are 'good', you are now ready to add fish!

A Guide to Cycling Your Tank

Step 6 - Bring Them Home!

After your tank has fully cycled, you are now ready to bring your fish home! Make sure that your tank is fully ready before purchasing your fish. When buying, be sure to check that your fish is healthy (no signs of out of place aggression, fins are full/no tears, no discoloration). Feel free to ask questions to the worker/person helping you!

Image Source: VasenkaPhotography - Flickr - CC

Fish are typically sent home in a bag. Before adding your fish to your tank, float them in the bag for 15-20 minutes. After time has passed, you may net your fish out of the bag or cut the bag open to release them into your tank.

Observe your fish for the next hour for any signs of stress. Feed them the next following day. It is normal for fish to 'sit' or exhibit any additional signs of stress for the first few weeks as they get used to their new aquarium.

Congrats! You are now a proud fish owner! Make sure to test the water regularly and feed regularly while always keeping an eye on your fish. Commit to regular cleanings and watch out for signs of illness. In case your fish shows signs of illness, here is a guide to diagnosing and treatment. It is always best to prevent to avoid dealing with any illness and making sure your water is clean and healthy!
